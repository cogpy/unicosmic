Vice Presidents (VP) Roles in the Company
1. Operations VP (5000)
Level: Administrative
Primary Focus: Managing product work for hotel, mall, and office complexes.
Responsibilities:
Ensuring efficient operations across hotel, mall, and office complexes.
Supervising hotel managers, lease managers, personnel officers, and other supervisors.
Managing commitment, potential, and performance in operations.
Coordinating with maintenance, financial, local sales, and marketing supervisors​​.
2. Personnel VP (4000)
Level: Administrative
Primary Focus: Overseeing organization work for the entire company.
Responsibilities:
Managing personnel-related activities, including hiring, training, development, employee relations, and benefits.
Ensuring the company has the right staff and organizational structure to meet its goals.
Overseeing personnel supervisors, human development supervisors, staff benefits supervisors, and human relations.
Ensuring commitment, potential, and performance within the personnel department​​.
3. Construction Manager (500)
Level: Executive Supervisor
Primary Focus: Overseeing property development projects.
Responsibilities:
Supervising construction activities and managing general foremen.
Ensuring projects are completed on time, within budget, and to the required quality standards.
Overseeing crews for building, high-rise construction, utilities, roadway construction, and interior décor.
Managing construction schedules, quality control, equipment, and materials​​.
4. Design Manager (200)
Level: Executive Supervisor
Primary Focus: Managing design work for property development.
Responsibilities:
Leading the design team to create effective and innovative designs.
Coordinating with other departments to integrate design with overall project goals.
Supervising drafting chiefs, senior architects, and senior design engineers.
Overseeing design schedules, budgeting, and quality inspection for design-related work​​.
5. Financial VP (7000)
Level: Administrative
Primary Focus: Overseeing financial work for the entire company.
Responsibilities:
Managing accounting, budgeting, financial planning, profit distribution, and fiscal marketing.
Ensuring the company's financial health and compliance with financial regulations.
Supervising accounting, budgeting, finance, profit distribution, and fiscal marketing supervisors​​.
6. Sales VP (8000)
Level: Administrative
Primary Focus: Managing sales work for the entire company.
Responsibilities:
Developing and implementing sales strategies.
Overseeing local and international sales, sales policy, pricing, and forecasting.
Aiming to maximize sales revenue and market share.
Supervising property sales managers, international sales managers, and sales policy managers​​.
7. Marketing VP (1000)
Level: Administrative
Primary Focus: Overseeing undelegated high-level strategic marketing tasks.
Responsibilities:
Developing marketing strategies and conducting market assessments.
Ensuring the company's marketing efforts align with its overall objectives.
Handling executive-level marketing tasks and overseeing marketing managers and acquisition managers​​.
Summary
Each VP in the company plays a critical role in aligning their departments with the company's strategic goals. They are responsible for managing their respective areas to ensure efficiency, growth, and alignment with the overall mission and vision of the company. By working collaboratively with other VPs and department heads, they drive the company forward, ensuring commitment, potential, and performance across all levels of the organization.

What are the Development VP's duties?
Development VP's Duties
Development VP (2000)
Level: Administrative
Primary Focus: Managing development work for the whole company.
Responsibilities:
Overseeing and coordinating all development projects within the company.
Working closely with other departments to ensure the successful implementation of development strategies.
Ensuring alignment of development projects with the company’s strategic goals.
Supervising project managers and development teams to ensure timely and budget-compliant project completion​​.
Summary
The Development VP is pivotal in steering the company's development efforts, ensuring that all projects align with the broader strategic goals, and facilitating cross-departmental coordination to drive successful project execution. This role requires strong leadership, strategic planning, and effective collaboration with other VPs and department heads.
