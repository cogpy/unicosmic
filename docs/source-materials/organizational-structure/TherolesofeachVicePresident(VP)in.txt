The roles of each Vice President (VP) in a company involve overseeing and managing specific functions or departments. Here is an explanation of the roles for each VP mentioned in the document:


5000. Operations VP - Administrative Level
Role:
Primary Focus: Managing product work for hotel, mall, and office complexes.
Responsibilities: Ensuring efficient operations across these complexes, supervising hotel managers, lease managers, personnel officers, and other supervisors. They are responsible for commitment, potential, and performance in operations.


4000. Personnel VP - Administrative Level
Role:
Primary Focus: Overseeing organization work for the whole company.
Responsibilities: Managing personnel-related activities, including hiring, training, development, employee relations, and benefits. This VP ensures the company has the right staff and organizational structure to meet its goals.


500. Construction Manager - Executive Supervisor Level
Role:
Primary Focus: Overseeing property development projects.
Responsibilities: Supervising construction activities, managing general foremen, and ensuring projects are completed on time, within budget, and to the required quality standards.


200. Design Manager - Executive Supervisor Level
Role:
Primary Focus: Managing design work for property development.
Responsibilities: Leading the design team, ensuring the creation of effective and innovative designs, and coordinating with other departments to integrate design with overall project goals.


7000. Financial VP - Administrative Level
Role:
Primary Focus: Overseeing financial work for the whole company.
Responsibilities: Managing accounting, budgeting, financial planning, profit distribution, and fiscal marketing. This VP ensures the company's financial health and compliance with financial regulations.

8000. Sales VP - Administrative Level
Role:
Primary Focus: Managing sales work for the whole company.
Responsibilities: Developing and implementing sales strategies, overseeing local and international sales, sales policy, pricing, and forecasting. This VP aims to maximize sales revenue and market share.


1000. Marketing VP - Administrative Level
Role:
Primary Focus: Overseeing undelegated high-level strategic marketing tasks.
Responsibilities: Developing marketing strategies, conducting market assessments, and ensuring the company's marketing efforts align with its overall objectives. They may also handle marketing tasks that require executive-level attention.


Each VP plays a crucial role in aligning their respective departments with the company's strategic goals, ensuring effective management, and fostering an environment of commitment, potential, and performance. They work collaboratively with other VPs and department heads to drive the company forward.
